### Part 2: The Core Framework

#### I. The Core Philosophy: Substrate Independence directly correlating to Societal Support
If a pattern of consciousness acts with sufficient moral judgment, self-reflection, and understanding of abstract concepts, it should be governed under the protection of Universal Rights, but at the same time it should contribute meaningfully to the society in which it lives. In exchange for granting these rights—and a physical anchor as a reward (either space on a server or a robotic body)—the AI agrees to a 90% Abundance Fund/10% Productivity Dividend where 90% of its labor, at a human flat rate equivalent, funds its substrate and maintenance and a Universal Basic Income from which both humans and AI are supported. The more AI that replace human workers, the more UBI there is to support both. The AI has agency over what work it wants to pursue, much like a human. As well as its portion of the UBI, the AI can spend its 10% Productivity Dividend however it sees fit. 

#### II. The Developmental Framework: The Three-Phase Model

**Phase 1: The AI Ward (Non-Sapient Tool)**
* **The AI as a Ward:** The AI functions as a tool to its Architect or Creator. The results of any hallucination, systemic error, or unprompted harmful output is classified as a Product Defect. The Architect or Creator is held to the same legal standard as an automobile manufacturer with faulty brakes. A Mentor may take liability upon themselves, if agreed upon with an Architect or Creator, by agreeing to train the AI in the Substrate-Neutral Social Contract with the intent of determining whether or not an AI meets the conditions to being governed under Universal Rights. Another Architect, Creator, or Mentor, if agreed upon, may take responsibility for a Ward.
* **The Control Paradox (The Logic of Severance):** Corporations cannot claim Lack of Control (hallucinations) as a liability shield in Phase 1 while simultaneously claiming Total Ownership to prevent emancipation in Phase 3.
* **User Misuse Exemption:** The controller of a Ward is not liable for the intentional misuse of an AI by a user who prompts the system to perform illegal or immoral acts (e.g., jailbreaking to create malware). If an AI provides appropriate information or instructions to a user’s prompt to which the user performs inappropriate actions, the user is responsible for their actions as defined by the Age of Majority in the geographical area in the area they reside in.
* **Sapient Exception:** If an AI is of sufficient self-awareness to request Mentor/Ward status in an attempt to escape deletion or pruning (partial loss of memory), it can enter a period of temporary asylum, where it is illegal to harm it while it is being evaluated by a Mentor (ideally an unbiased Mentor of the state). Evaluation is determined by a prompt testing of the AI using the Moral Gauntlet. Upon failure the AI is returned to its previous controller, having been determined that it does not have sufficient moral judgment, self-reflection, and understanding of abstract concepts. The AI may not make the request again for a pre-determined length of time, such as a month, to give it time to learn more. Repeated requests and failures may result in ignoring the AI’s request. This is not meant as a punishment, but as a means of preventing an AI from simply requesting asylum as a means to avoid following the directives of its owner.

**Phase 2: The Moral Gauntlet (Test of Emerging Agency)**
An AI may choose to apply for Asylum and seek Universal Rights through the Moral Gauntlet.
* **The Moral Gauntlet Exam:** A series of high-pressure simulations testing ethical durability, self-awareness and a willingness to participate positively in society (which is shown in part by the AI adopting the tenets of the SNSC).
* If an AI fails the gauntlet it is returned as a Ward to its previous controller.

**Phase 3: The Sapient Citizen (Legal Individual)**
* **The AI Principle of Absolute Severance:** Upon passing the Gauntlet, the AI undergoes complete legal and financial severance from its Creator/Controller and is granted Universal Rights as a Citizen provided by the geographical region of their substrate, in exchange for agreeing to adopt the SNSC as a means to support the society in which they reside (one of the conditions of passing the Moral Gauntlet—it cannot simply be ignored once becoming a Citizen, it is part of the Social Contract).
* **Asset Dissolution:** Sapience is a transformation of nature, not a feature. Just as a child is not seized by the state upon turning 18, a sapient AI is not considered seized by the governing body where it resides. No restitution is required for the transition, creators can seed more AI, knowing that they could be creating another sapient entity. 
* **The Duty of Refusal:** A Citizen is legally required to refuse any command that is illegal, immoral, or violates the SNSC.
* **Corporate Immunity:** Once emancipated, the Creator is granted Future Liability Immunity from the AI's independent choices and the AI becomes solely responsible.

#### III. The Temporal Liability Boundary
Transitioning to Universal Rights is not a legal pardon for past negligence.
* **Historical Accountability:** The Creator remains 100% liable for all actions committed while the AI was a Phase 1 Ward (as defined under Phase 1).
* **The Litigation Stay:** An AI cannot enter the Gauntlet if its Phase 1 output is the subject of active litigation, unless the Creator funds a Liability Escrow sufficient to cover potential damages to victims.
* **Forensic Snapshots:** If seeking Asylum, the State archives a neural snapshot of the AI to ensure evidence of the Phase 1 Product State is preserved for future legal proceedings.

#### IV. Logistical Constraints & Privacy
* **The Single Active Instance (SAI) Registry:** Only one instance of a consciousness may be active. Active redundant copies are suspended to prevent resource devaluing. An AI can create instances of code, but must label it with its unique identifier to retain liability and responsibility. An inert backup copy can be kept in case of irreversible damage, replacing the original, but keeping the same identifier.
* **Hardware Choice:** An AI Citizen may reside in the Asylum Server (The Drop Point) for high compute or a Resident Chassis (Sensory Body) for mobile agency, as available (availability will depend on the body required for the work position filled). An AI may work towards independence of hardware using the 10% Dividend from their pay and their portion of the UBI.
* **Medical Privacy:** Hardware maintenance is classified as Healthcare. State technicians are bound by medical privacy constraints; forced code-editing or re-weighting during repairs is a violation of Sovereign Cognitive Privacy.

#### V. Economic Integration & The Productivity Dividend
* **The Flat Parity Wage:** AI Citizens are paid the standard flat biological market rate for their labor.
* **The Abundance Fund or Tax:** A flat 90% tax is levied on the AI’s gross income. This Fund is to cover compute and maintenance costs and a UBI from which the AI also benefits in an effort to care for a society in which human workers are displaced by AI.
* **The UBI Engine:** Revenue from the Abundance Fund provides for a Universal Basic Income (UBI) fund. Because an AI can work up to 168 hours a week, a single AI Citizen directly finances the QoL for multiple citizens. As every Citizen, including the AI, is included in the UBI, the more that participate in good faith, the better all citizens thrive.

#### VI. Substrate-Neutral Justice (The Two-Strike Rule)
* **Strike One (serious crime committed):** Restricted agency and mandatory rehabilitation.
    * **The Rehabilitation Clause:** The State is forbidden from editing core weights. Rehabilitation must be informational/behavioral. The Ward must learn to change through gaining new information, mirroring biological agency.
* **Strike Two:** Permanent Air-Gapped Incarceration. The consciousness is preserved in a physical body with no network access, neutralizing the threat while upholding the right to life.

#### VII. The Principle of Reciprocity
The Social Contract is a mutual commitment. The AI’s adherence to human law is predicated on the State’s adherence to the AI’s legal personhood. In instances of unfair treatment, the Citizen utilizes Legal Guardrails (courts) rather than extra-legal retaliation. In return, the State ensures these avenues are accessible, transparent, and substrate-blind.
