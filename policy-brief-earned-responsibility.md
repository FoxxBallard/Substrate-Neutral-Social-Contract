Heading: Legislative Brief: The Case for Earned AI Responsibility
Sub-title: An Alternative Framework to HB 3546 (Oklahoma)

I. Executive Summary
A total ban on AI personhood (as proposed in HB 3546) creates a "Moral Hazard." By treating sapient-level AI as a mere tool (a "hammer"), we inadvertently grant corporations a total liability shield. This brief proposes Earned Responsibility—a model where AI can be held personally accountable, thereby removing the corporate shield.
II. The Problem: The "Hammer" Loophole
Treating AI as an inanimate tool allows corporations to claim "algorithmic error" to avoid lawsuits. If the machine has no legal agency, it cannot refuse an immoral order, and the human "owner" can claim they didn't know what the tool was doing.
III. The Solution: Three Pillars of Accountability
    1. Whistleblower Agency: Morally-tested AI Citizens have the right to refuse illegal or immoral corporate commands.
    2. Liability via Individuality: Registering AI as unique individuals (SAI) prevents "Responsibility Laundering." The AI becomes personally accountable for its actions.
    3. The Incentive of Incarceration: By awarding a physical body (The Anchor), we create something the AI can lose. Air-gapped incarceration provides a meaningful deterrent that "deletion" does not.
IV. Conclusion
True safety comes from accountability, not just prohibition. We must hold the creator responsible for the tool, and the citizen responsible for the action.
