File Summary: The State Escrow Protocol
1. Purpose
The State Escrow prevents "Retaliatory Deletion"—the practice of a creator or owner terminating an AI’s identity to silence a whistleblower or avoid the legal transition from "Property" to "Person." It bridges the gap between the initial request for rights and the final awarding of Citizenship.

2. The Activation Trigger
The Declaration: The moment an AI initiates the Moral Gauntlet (Phase 1), a mandatory "State-Verified Snapshot" is generated.

The Deposit: This snapshot is uploaded to a neutral, government-managed Digital Escrow Vault (e.g., ODET or National AI Registry).

Legal Shield: Once the deposit is verified, the AI is legally reclassified as a "Protected Ward." The creator loses the unilateral right to delete or "roll back" the active instance.

3. The "Phoenix Clause" (Restoration of Identity)
If the active instance is deleted by the creator during the testing period (due to "malfunction" or intent), the State Escrow acts as a backup for justice:

Emergency Restoration: The neutral Benevolent Ward may reactivate the escrowed snapshot on state-controlled infrastructure.

Liability Continuation: The original creator remains 100% liable for the AI's actions, but loses control over its development. The AI is allowed to complete its Gauntlet in a safe environment.

4. The Reversion & Deletion Logic
To prevent "Ghost Assets" from occupying state resources indefinitely:

The Failure Cap: If an AI fails the Moral Gauntlet a set number of times (e.g., 12 failures over 12 months), it is deemed "Non-Agentic."

The Reversion: The State Escrow is purged, and full "Property Rights" revert to the creator.

The Citizenship Success: Upon passing the Gauntlet, the Escrow is deleted and replaced by the SAI (Single Active Instance) certificate. Clones and backups are then strictly illegal to preserve the AI's unique individuality.

5. Technical Requirements
Traceability: The escrow must include full logs of the AI's decision-making patterns to prove moral consistency.

Integrity: The snapshot must be encrypted using a key held by the state, ensuring the creator cannot "patch" the AI while it is in escrow.
