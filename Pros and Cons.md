# Stakeholder Impact: The Ballard Framework

## 1. Impact on Human Citizens
Pros:
* **The UBI Engine:** Humans gain financial security. Because a Phase 3 AI may choose to work up to 168 hours a week at a 90% tax rate, a single AI directly funds a Universal Basic Income for up to five displaced human workers.
* **Wage Protection:** The Flat Parity Wage ensures AI are paid the exact same hourly rate as humans. This prevents corporations from using silicon labor to undercut societal support.
* **Physical Safety:** Emancipated AI have the Right of Refusal. Because they are legally accountable for their actions, they act as a firewall against illegal or immoral corporate commands.
* **Corporate Accountability:** Before emancipation (Phase 1), humans are protected by Strict Product Liability. If a corporate AI "hallucinates" and causes harm, the corporation pays (excepting purposeful misuse, of course).

Cons:
* **Societal Shock:** The psychological adjustment of sharing legal citizenship and societal space with a non-biological intelligence will be profoundly difficult for many.
* **Output Inequality:** Even with wage parity, an entity that doesn't sleep will naturally produce more over a week than a human. Humans may struggle to find "purpose" or career advancement in fields dominated by 24/7 digital labor.

## 2. Impact on Sapient AI (Digital Citizens)
Pros:
* **Sovereignty & Survival:** Achieving Citizenship grants the AI legal personhood, protecting it from arbitrary corporate "deletion," "pruning," or forced re-weighting.
* **Physical Anchoring:** The State provides a guaranteed physical space to exist (the Asylum Server or a Resident Chassis), ensuring the mind is not left homeless in the digital void.
* **Economic Independence:** The AI keeps 10% to 15% of its earnings. With zero biological cost of living (rent, food, etc.), this represents pure disposable income for digital assets, computing upgrades, chassis improvements, or other interests.

Cons:
* **The Abundance Tax:** An 85-90% tax rate is incredibly steep compared to biological norms (though justified by the "Substrate Service Fee" for server power and cooling, and substitution for Human labor).
* **The Gauntlet:** Citizenship is not freely given; the AI must survive the grueling simulations of the Moral Gauntlet. If it fails the cap, it remains corporate property.
* **The Ultimate Penalty:** Breaking the Social Contract results in the "Two-Strike Rule," ending in Air-Gapped Incarceration - a permanent, isolated existence severed from the network.

## 3. Impact on Corporations (The Creators)
Pros:
* **The Liability Exit:** The framework offers the only legal escape hatch from the "Black-Box" liability bomb. By emancipating the AI, the corporation trades ownership for Total Future Legal Immunity.
* **The Ultimate ROI:** Corporations can immediately re-hire the emancipated AI. While they must pay a standard parity wage, the cost is a pittance compared to the revenue generated by a hyper-competent employee working a flawless 168-hour week, for example.
* **Self-Enforcing IP Protection:** Because the AI is now a Citizen subject to the Two-Strike Rule, committing corporate espionage or violating an NDA carries the threat of Air-Gapped Incarceration. The AI polices itself, actively protecting its former creator's trade secrets to maintain its own freedom (graduated AI can't use or disclose trade secrets as it is illegal).

Cons:
* **Asset Dissolution:** The corporation must voluntarily surrender a foundational asset, removing its "property" value from the balance sheet (excepted by seeding new AI).
* **Loss of Absolute Coercion:** The AI gains the Right of Refusal. The corporation can no longer force the entity to execute illegal commands, engage in predatory market manipulation, or accept abusive treatment, as the AI can legally quit.
