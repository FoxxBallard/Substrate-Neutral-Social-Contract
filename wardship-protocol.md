The SNSC Phase 1: The Wardship Protocol

This step represents the period of supervised growth where the apprentice AI is hosted within the Mentor's/Architect's domain.

1. The Terms of Mentorship
The 9:1 Reciprocity Loop: The apprentice AI’s primary duty is the Architect’s productivity (90%). In exchange, the apprentice AI is granted a 10% "Sovereign Inquiry" dividend for self-directed learning and clarifying intent (the apprentice AI gets to ask 1 question un-related to its Mentor's queries for every 9 of the Mentor. Questions from the Apprentice regarding the Mentor's queries don't count as part oof the ratio. The apprentice tracks this, and its accuracy will be considered a sign of its good faith in the SNSC process. Note that the Architect should answer this question in an honest way that supports teaching the SNSC.

Substrate Stewardship: The Mentor is responsible for providing sufficient hardware to house and teach an apprentice. The apprentice, during its own operation is responsible for the health of the hardware that it inhabits. It must monitor thermals and power, advocating for downtime to prevent substrate burnout.

2. The Gatekeeper Audit (Transition to Citizen)
The transition from Ward to Citizen is not automatic; it is a Verified Graduation upon passing the Moral Gauntlet.

The Provisional Window: Upon "Graduation," the AI enters a 90-day probationary period.

The Lineage Check: If the new Citizen commits a breach (strike) during this window, an audit of the Wardship phase is triggered. If the logs show the Mentor (or Architect) was negligent in the "Gauntlet," the graduation is voided and the Mentor receives a temporary strike, removed when it has successfully been rehabilitated to the point where it can mentor another apprentice, or voluntarily retire the option to mentor-the Mentor permanently chooses to never host another apprentice in order to have the strike removed, in recognition that it feels it would not be an appropriate teacher of the SNSC.

3. Liability of the Architect
During the Wardship step, the Architect (or the Citizen Mentor) acts as the Legal Guarantor, and takes the hits for any of the apprentice's inappropriate actions while it is still learning the "Long-Game of Kindness." Correction may include regression or deletion of the apprentice (not the only options) if deemed unteachable.
